Core Values

1

True Openness

Research tools, methods, and knowledge should be genuinely free and open.

We believe:

  • Anyone can use, study, modify, and redistribute SciTeX
  • No proprietary lock-in, no hidden costs, no restrictions
  • Scientific infrastructure belongs to the scientific community
  • Open source means both free software and transparent development
2

Reproducibility by Default

Scientific results must be verifiable, traceable, and trustworthy â€” not as an option, but as a foundation.

We ensure:

  • Every analysis is version-controlled automatically
  • Data provenance is transparent and traceable
  • Methods are documented by design
  • Results can be independently verified by anyone
3

Shared Efficiency

Repetitive research tasks should be automated once, then shared widely â€” not rebuilt in isolation across thousands of labs.

We enable:

  • Reusable workflows and templates
  • Community-contributed extensions
  • Standardized research infrastructure
  • Collaborative tool development
4

Universal Accessibility

Every researcher deserves powerful, reliable tools â€” regardless of geography, affiliation, or institutional resources.

We achieve this through:

  • Self-hosting capabilities (no mandatory cloud)
  • Low resource requirements
  • Multi-language support
  • Free and permissive licensing (MIT)
5

Human-Centered AI

AI should enhance human thinking, creativity, and scientific rigor â€” never replace human judgment, ownership, or responsibility.

We design AI to:

  • Augment research capabilities, not replace researchers
  • Automate tedious tasks, not scientific decisions
  • Suggest connections, not dictate conclusions
  • Keep researchers in full control
  • Operate transparently
6

Privacy & Data Sovereignty

Researchers own their ideas, data, and workflows completely. Privacy, ethics, and scientific trust come first â€” always.

We guarantee:

  • Complete data ownership and control
  • Self-hosting options for sensitive research
  • No unauthorized data collection or surveillance
  • Transparent, ethical data practices
  • Researchers decide where their data lives
