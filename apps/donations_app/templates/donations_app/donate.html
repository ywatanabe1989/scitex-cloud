Why Your Support Matters

SciTeX is built by researchers for researchers. Unlike commercial scientific platforms, we don't charge subscription fees or lock features behind paywalls.

Your donations directly support:

  • Hosting costs for our cloud infrastructure
  • Development time for new features and improvements
  • Documentation to help researchers use our platform
  • Community support to assist users with questions
  • Integration with journals and research databases

Open Source Commitment

SciTeX is committed to remaining 100% open source. All our code is available on GitHub under the MIT license.

As an alternative to monetary donations, we also welcome code contributions, bug reports, and feature requests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my donation tax-deductible?
Yes, in many countries donations to open-source scientific projects qualify as tax-deductible contributions. We provide receipts for all donations.
Can I set up a recurring donation?
Yes, after selecting your donation amount, you'll have the option to make it a one-time or monthly recurring donation.
How are donations used?
Donations directly support development, infrastructure costs, and community support. We publish quarterly financial reports detailing how funds are allocated.
Can my university or institution sponsor SciTeX?
Absolutely! We welcome institutional sponsorships. Please contact us at sponsorships@scitex.ai to discuss partnership opportunities.
I can't donate money. How else can I help?
Contributing code, reporting bugs, improving documentation, or simply spreading the word about SciTeX are all valuable ways to support the project.
